Welcome to my blog & portfolio

Hi, my name is Daniel and I am a Computer Engineering student in the Alicante's universitity.

The goal of this blog is to create a space where I can share all the projects I work on and the solutions I develop for LeetCode challenges, accompanied by clear and detailed explanations. It also serves as my personal portfolio & a place to showcase my growth as a computer engineer, reflect on what I learn along the way, and hopefully contribute to others who are on a similar path.

About Me

I'm a tech enthusiast who enjoys breaking down complex problems and building smart, elegant solutions. I'm currently a first-year Computer Engineering student, still early in my journey, but already deeply passionate about building things that are useful and efficient. What draws me to software development is the problem solving process it feels like tackling complex puzzles, only more rewarding and with real world impact.

I mainly work with C and C++, and I use Python for math-related tasks. I've also worked with MySQL, and recently started learning Rust out of personal interest, attracted by its focus on performance and memory safety. Java is on the horizon as part of my university curriculum.

Most of what I know, I've learned through curiosity and persistence reading, experimenting, and figuring things out by myself. I often rely on resources like Stack Overflow or AI tools to refine my understanding or explore new ideas, but I always aim to keep the process hands-on and meaningful. This very website is a small example of that approach: every detail here is something I've designed, questioned, and improved along the way.

I don't yet have a fixed path within tech, but I'm particularly interested in application development both desktop and mobile and in building highly optimized and clean backends. I believe it's an area that often gets overlooked but makes a huge difference in real world performance.
